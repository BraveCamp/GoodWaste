Not Enough Done to Help the Environment

Getting rid of unused plastic waste is one of the best steps to take toward a better environment.
The amount of plastic waste not properly dispensed continues to grow on a daily basis and have devastating effects on our ecosystems, health, and much more:

  • 34.4% was the recycle rate of 2013 within the U.S.
  • 8 million metric tons of plastic get in oceans every year
  • Plastic is 90% of all trash on the ocean surface
  • About 5.25 trillion pieces of plastic are in the ocean

There are Consequences

It is important to address the issue of rising levels of plastic waste since it has harmful consequences:

  • Businesses would produce more bottles to replace those not recycled to keep up with demand
  • Plastic kills about 100,000 marine creatures annually from entanglement (only the ones found)
  • Most plastic is made from fossil fuels which releases toxins into the air

Recycling the Usual Way can be Harmful

One might turn to doing the conventional approach to recycling by putting plastic materials into the recycle bin. However, that is not the best decision:

  • It can harm workers and communities. Most recycled plastic goes to manufacturing companies that melt the plastic which releases harmful toxins or VOCs (volatile organic compounds) that can lead to health problems and other issues.
  • It contributes to global warming because when they are melted by plastic manufacturing companies, they also release greenhouse gases that degenerate the ozone layer.
